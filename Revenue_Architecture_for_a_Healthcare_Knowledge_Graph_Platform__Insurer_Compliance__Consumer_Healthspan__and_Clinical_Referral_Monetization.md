# Revenue Architecture for the Healthcare Knowledge Garden

## Three Interconnected Revenue Engines

**Platform:** Healthcare Knowledge Garden (HKG) — 12.1M+ records, 9.4M NPI providers, 28-table schema, 17+ federal data sources
**Research Date:** April 12, 2026
**Combined Addressable Spend:** $250B+ across three engines

---

## Engine 1: Insurance / Payer B2B ($25–30M ARR potential, 3–5 years)

### Regulatory Hammer Creating Mandatory Demand

The No Surprises Act (effective January 2022) requires health plans to verify and update provider directory data every 90 days, update online directories within 48 hours of receiving changes, and respond to patient inquiries about network status within one business day. Violations carry fines of up to $10,000 per violation, and CMS can halt a plan's ability to enroll new members for an entire plan year.

The ghost network problem is severe. ProPublica found that 86% of listed mental health providers in New York were unreachable, not in-network, or not accepting new patients. Arizona regulators couldn't schedule visits with nearly 2 out of every 5 providers called. California has issued only a single fine ($7,500) despite ghost network regulation since 2016.

Enforcement is tightening. The bipartisan REAL Health Providers Act would require Medicare Advantage plans to update provider directories annually starting 2026. Starting 2025, payers in state-based exchanges must meet network adequacy standards, expanding to all state-based exchange plans by 2026.

### Market Size: $4.7–5.8B

Provider data management market: $1.5–2.5B core directory accuracy segment. Broader healthcare payer network management: $4.7–5.8B growing at 9–15% CAGR. CAQH estimates the healthcare industry spends $83B annually on administrative transactions with a remaining $21B savings opportunity.

### Pricing Models

Per-provider-per-year (PPPY): $1.68 (CAQH) to $3–10 (premium verification). 9.4M NPI records at $3/provider = $28.2M annually. API per-query: $0.01–$0.50/query. Enterprise SaaS: $100K–$5M+/year (Verato averages ~$400K/client). PMPM: $0.05–$0.50 per member per month. Compliance modules: $50K–$250K/year add-ons.

### Telehealth Integration Economics

Teladoc Health: $2.53B revenue, 83% from recurring PMPM/PEPM access fees. Amwell: $249.3M ($132.4M subscriptions). Insurers pay access fees, not per-referral. Telehealth parity: 23–24 states mandate same reimbursement. CMS permanent telehealth codes. Consumer visit prices: $29–79 (Sesame/Costco) to $49 (Amazon One Medical). Blended insurer reimbursement: ~$140/visit.

## Engine 3: High-Value Medical Service Referrals ($1K–$10K+ per conversion)

### Clinical Trial Matching (Highest Value)

Pharma pays $15,000–50,000 per recruited patient. Patient recruitment = 32% of total trial costs (single largest driver). Median per-patient trial cost: $41,117. Phase III: $113,000–136,000. 80% of trials delayed by recruitment. 512,000+ ongoing trials worldwide.

Market: $3.5–11.8B in 2025, projected $26B+ by 2035. Companies: Antidote Technologies (pay-per-randomized-patient), SubjectWell (OneView portal, Feb 2025).

HKG advantage: 33K clinical trials and 60K PubMed citations already loaded. Knowledge graph can match patients to appropriate trials based on conditions, medications, and eligibility criteria.

### Regenerative Medicine & Longevity Clinics (Cash-Pay, Zero AKS Exposure)

| Service | Price Range | Referral Opportunity |
|---------|------------|---------------------|
| NAD+ IV therapy | $250–1,500/session | $50–300/referral |
| Stem cell therapy | $5,000–50,000/treatment | $500–5,000/referral |

| Peptide therapy | $200–2,000/month | $40–400/referral |

Clinic acquisition costs: $500–3,000+ per patient. HKG referral fee: $200–2,000/patient. Cash-pay = zero anti-kickback risk.

### Concierge Medicine

Market: $20.4–23.6B in 2025, projected $39–48B by 2033. MDVIP: $1,800–5,000/year membership, 90% retention, 1,300+ physicians, 400,000+ patients. 5-year LTV: $10,800–22,500. Referral fee: $200–2,000/member.

### Medical Tourism

Facilitators earn 10–40% commissions on $5K–50K procedures ($500–10,000/referral). Market: $34–91B. 1.4M US patients travel annually. 60% motivated by 50–65% domestic cost differentials.

### Telehealth Marketplaces

Zocdoc: $35–110 per new patient booking. Sesame: $29 virtual care via Costco partnership. Take rates: 15–25%. OIG reviewed Zocdoc's booking fee structure and found it AKS-compliant.

## Strategic Sequencing

1. **Build insurer B2B base** — Mandatory compliance spend creates reliable recurring revenue and institutional credibility
2. **Scale free consumer tools** — Gravity Well pulls millions of health-intent users at zero acquisition cost
3. **Layer high-value referrals** — Clinical trials, longevity clinics, concierge medicine on top of qualified user base

Each layer reinforces the others: insurer data accuracy improves consumer experience, consumer scale attracts premium providers, knowledge graph comprehensiveness creates a moat point solutions cannot replicate.
