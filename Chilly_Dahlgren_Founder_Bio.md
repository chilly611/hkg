## The Origin Story (Lead With This)

Before there were large language models, before there were knowledge graphs, before "AI" was a consumer product — there was Kathleen Dahlgren, and she was teaching her 10-year-old son to code in machine language on an Apple IIe.

Kathleen Dahlgren was a pioneer of computational linguistics — the science of teaching computers to understand human language. She earned her PhD in Linguistics and a post-doctorate in Computer Science from UCLA. Her book *Naive Semantics for Natural Language Understanding* was published by MIT Press in 1988 and reprinted three times. It proposed something radical for its era: that computers could understand language not through rigid rules, but through "naive theories" — commonsense knowledge about how the world actually works. Word meanings aren't dictionary definitions. They're webs of intuitive knowledge about objects, events, and relationships.

She was a Senior Scientist at IBM in the 1980s. She served as an Adjunct Professor at UCLA for 14 years. She founded Cognition Technologies in Santa Monica in the early 1990s, where her team built a natural-language search engine that — in the words of her obituary — "foreshadowed some of the capabilities of recent artificial intelligence methods." That company and its massive ontology (described as representing 40,000 PhD-person-years of structured knowledge) was acquired by Nuance Communications, where she served as Principal Scientist and Senior Manager.

She was a member of AAAI in the 1970s — when artificial intelligence was still a fringe academic pursuit. She taught her son about neural networks, about ontologies, about the structure of knowledge itself. She showed him that intelligence — human or machine — is built from relationships between concepts, not from isolated facts.

Kathleen Dahlgren passed away in 2024. Her son, Charles, is now building Knowledge Gardens.

**The Knowledge Garden architecture — a knowledge graph that connects every entity in a trillion-dollar industry through structured relationships, powering AI-native intelligence — is the direct descendant of Kathleen Dahlgren's life's work.** The ontology became a garden. The naive semantics became the AI layer. The commonsense knowledge became the Morning Briefing. The roots run deep.
