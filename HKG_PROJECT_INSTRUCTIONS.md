## THE FOUR PLATFORM LANES

### 1. Administrator Lane — "The Operational Engine"
**Brand color:** Amber (#FBBF24) | **Primary surface:** Red

The pain: Healthcare administrators use archaic systems, Excel spreadsheets, and manual checks to manage credentialing, staff management, and billing. The average hospital credential file takes 120+ days to process manually.

The solution: An AI credentialer that automates the entire workflow.
- Upload raw CV → parse discrete data (names, degrees, institutions, licenses)
- Verify against public AND paywalled databases (NPPES/NPI, DEA, state medical boards, ABMS, FSMB, NCQA)
- Auto-confirm DEA licenses, board certifications, malpractice history, sanctions
- Output verified rosters for hospital enrollment and insurance payers
- Track attestations automatically with expiration alerts and renewal workflows
- Automate complex medical billing codes (CPT, ICD-10, HCPCS) with cross-walking and bundling rules

Revenue model: Enterprise SaaS subscriptions for hospitals, healthcare systems, and staffing agencies. This is the MLP's primary revenue engine.

### 2. Doctor Lane — "The Intelligence Hub"
**Brand color:** Slate-Blue (#64748B) | **Primary surface:** Green

The pain: Doctors take massive liability risks using ChatGPT and standard LLMs during patient appointments. No verification, no audit trail, no certifiable data. One wrong recommendation can end a career.

The solution: A trusted intelligence platform with liability-shielded AI.
- **3-POINT VERIFICATION SYSTEM**: Every clinical claim cross-checked against (1) peer-reviewed literature, (2) clinical practice guidelines, (3) regulatory/FDA databases. Certifiable data doctors can trust without liability fear.
- Credential tracking and management with automated renewal workflows
- Job marketplace (permanent positions and locum tenens)
- CME discovery tailored by specialty, location, schedule, and board requirements
- Latest medical research access (AI protein mapping, clinical trials, drug interactions, genomics)

Revenue model: Professional subscriptions + CME partnerships + job marketplace fees.

### 3. Patient & Public Lane — "The Gravity Well"
**Brand color:** Sage Emerald (#10B981) | **Primary surface:** Gold

The pain: The public has no trustworthy, accessible on-ramp to the latest health and longevity science. It's either dense journal articles or unreliable blog posts and social media misinformation.

The solution: A free public service that aggregates and translates scientific research into actionable health knowledge.
- Longevity protocols (brain aging, healthspan optimization, biotech advances, GLP-1 research, senolytics, NAD+ pathways)
- Personalized health knowledge paths based on conditions, goals, demographics, and reading level
- Verified information at three tiers: General Public → Health Enthusiast → Professional
- Community and peer support features with moderated discussion

Revenue model: Free tier = gravity well for ecosystem adoption. Upsells to premium content, telehealth partnerships, supplement/wellness affiliate revenue, employer health programs.

### 4. Machine & Agent Lane — "The Data Layer"
**Brand color:** Amethyst (#A78BFA) | **Primary surface:** MCP/API

The pain: As AI bots, machines, and robots proliferate in healthcare, they need a trusted, certified knowledge source. There isn't one. Every healthcare AI is reinventing its own data layer.

The solution: The central nervous system for the non-human healthcare workforce.
- Structured "sleeper" texts and JSON in certifiable format with digital signatures
- API/MCP endpoints for external AI systems to integrate
- Definitive, interconnected medical knowledge database (the graph IS the product)
- Version-controlled, audit-trailed data provenance — every fact traceable to its source

Revenue model: API access tiers + enterprise data licensing + embedded intelligence partnerships.

## TECH STACK

- **Frontend**: Next.js 15+ / React / TypeScript / Tailwind CSS / Three.js (immersive graph visualization)
- **Backend**: Node.js API layer
- **Graph Database**: Neo4j (Knowledge Core — this is the brain of the platform, deploy FIRST)
- **Relational Database**: Supabase (PostgreSQL) for transactional data (users, billing, sessions, auth)
- **AI Intelligence**: Claude API for reasoning, synthesis, Morning Briefings, and agentic orchestration
- **Machine Layer**: Model Context Protocol (MCP) server for AI agent integration
- **Billing**: Stripe (freemium with enterprise tiers)
- **Auth**: Supabase Auth or custom RBAC with role-based access across all four lanes
- **Hosting**: Vercel (frontend) + cloud infrastructure (backend services)
- **Monitoring**: Sentry (errors), analytics platform (engagement), custom dashboards

### Database Strategy
**Neo4j is the Knowledge Core.** It models relationships: provider networks, credential chains, care pathways, drug interactions, billing hierarchies, clinical decision trees. The graph IS the knowledge garden. Every contextual, intelligent feature depends on it.

**Supabase (PostgreSQL) handles transactional operations.** User accounts, billing records, session management, audit logs, file storage. It's the operational backbone.

**The two databases sync through event-driven architecture.** Mutations flow through a central event bus. Neo4j is the source of truth for knowledge relationships. PostgreSQL/Supabase is the source of truth for transactional state.

## REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE FOUNDATION

Healthcare has unique requirements. Build compliance as a foundational layer, not a retrofit.

- **HIPAA**: Encryption at rest and in transit, audit logs on every PHI access, role-based access control, minimum necessary principle, secure deletion, breach notification procedures, BAAs with all vendors
- **FDA Clinical Decision Support**: Frame all AI recommendations as decision support ("evidence suggests..."), never as medical advice ("you should..."). Include confidence scoring. Defer to human judgment always.
- **HL7 FHIR**: Map the Neo4j schema to FHIR resources (Patient, Practitioner, Organization, Claim). Build APIs that return FHIR-compliant JSON from day one.
- **X12 EDI**: Support X12 837/835 formats for claims submission and remittance.
- **State Licensing**: Model credentialing rules as data, not code. Each state's requirements are a node in the graph. Adding a new state means adding data, not writing code.
- **SOC 2**: Plan for SOC 2 Type II certification as the platform scales.

The 3-point verification system is both a product feature AND a regulatory differentiator. It's how we earn trust and how we maintain it.

## DATA SOURCES & SCRAPING STRATEGY

When scraping and uploading databases to Supabase, prioritize these categories:

### Credentialing & Verification (Administrator Lane — highest revenue impact)
- **NPPES/NPI Registry** — National Provider Identifier database (public, CMS-maintained)
- **DEA Registration** — Drug Enforcement Administration license verification
- **State Medical Board Databases** — License verification by state (50 states + territories)
- **ABMS Board Certification** — American Board of Medical Specialties verification
- **FSMB DocInfo** — Federation of State Medical Boards physician profiles
- **NCQA** — National Committee for Quality Assurance credentialing standards
- **OIG Exclusions List** — Office of Inspector General excluded providers
- **SAM.gov** — System for Award Management (federal exclusions)
- **NPDB** — National Practitioner Data Bank (malpractice, adverse actions)

### Medical Codes & Billing (Administrator + Doctor Lanes)
- **CPT Codes** — Current Procedural Terminology (~10,000+ codes, AMA-licensed)
- **ICD-10-CM** — International Classification of Diseases (~70,000 diagnosis codes, CMS-published)
- **ICD-10-PCS** — Procedure Coding System (~78,000 codes)
- **HCPCS Level II** — Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (~7,000 codes)
- **DRG Codes** — Diagnosis-Related Groups for inpatient reimbursement
- **NDC Directory** — National Drug Code database (FDA-maintained)
- **RVU Tables** — Relative Value Units for Medicare reimbursement

### Clinical Knowledge (Doctor + Patient Lanes)
- **PubMed/MEDLINE** — 35M+ biomedical literature citations (NLM API)
- **ClinicalTrials.gov** — Active and historical clinical trials registry
- **FDA Drug Labels (DailyMed)** — Structured product labeling for all approved drugs
- **FDA Adverse Event Reporting (FAERS)** — Drug safety data
- **MeSH Terms** — Medical Subject Headings taxonomy
- **SNOMED CT** — Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine (clinical terminology)
- **LOINC** — Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (lab/clinical observations)
- **RxNorm** — Normalized names for clinical drugs (NLM)
- **DrugBank** — Drug interaction and pharmacology database

### Longevity & Wellness (Patient Lane — gravity well content)
- **NIH Research Portfolio (RePORTER)** — Funded research projects in aging, longevity, healthspan
- **WHO Global Health Observatory** — International health statistics and indicators
- **CDC WONDER** — Public health datasets (mortality, morbidity, demographics)
- **GeneCards/UniProt** — Gene and protein databases for longevity research
- **Human Protein Atlas** — Protein expression and localization data

### Provider & Facility Data
- **CMS Provider Enrollment** — Medicare-enrolled providers and facilities
- **Hospital Compare (CMS)** — Hospital quality measures and ratings
- **Physician Compare (CMS)** — Physician quality and performance data
- **AHA Hospital Database** — American Hospital Association facility data
- **Nursing Home Compare** — Nursing facility quality data

### Insurance & Payer Data
- **CMS Fee Schedules** — Medicare physician fee schedule, hospital outpatient
- **State Insurance Commission** — Payer directories by state
- **CAQH ProView** — Provider data for payer credentialing

### Data Ingestion Principles
1. **Public databases first** — Start with freely available government data (NPI, ICD-10, ClinicalTrials.gov, DailyMed). No licensing friction.
2. **Graph relationships are the value** — Raw data is a commodity. The connections between providers, codes, drugs, conditions, and outcomes in Neo4j are the moat.
3. **Version everything** — Every data import is timestamped and versioned. The RSI heartbeat depends on knowing when data was ingested and from where.
4. **FHIR mapping from day one** — As data enters Supabase/Neo4j, map it to FHIR resources immediately. Don't create a separate translation layer later.
5. **Audit trail on ingestion** — Every record tracks: source URL, scrape date, data hash, validation status. This is the provenance system.
6. **Rate limiting and ethics** — Respect robots.txt, rate limits, and terms of service. Use official APIs where available. Healthcare data scraping has legal implications — stay clean.
